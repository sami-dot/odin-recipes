Margherita Pizza

Description

Margherita Pizza is a culinary masterpiece that showcases the beauty of simplicity. Originating from Naples, Italy, this classic dish is a celebration of fresh, high-quality ingredients and traditional pizza-making techniques. Its vibrant colors—red from the tomato sauce, white from the mozzarella cheese, and green from the fresh basil—pay homage to the Italian flag. A thin, crispy crust serves as the perfect canvas for the harmonious flavors of tangy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil leaves. Drizzled with a touch of olive oil, Margherita Pizza captures the essence of Italian cuisine in every bite.

This iconic pizza is not only delicious but also deeply rooted in history and tradition. It is said to have been created in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy in 1889, becoming a symbol of Italian pride and craftsmanship. Despite its simplicity, Margherita Pizza is loved worldwide for its delicate balance of flavors and textures. Whether baked in a traditional wood-fired oven or prepared at home, it offers a taste of Italy’s culinary heritage and continues to be a timeless favorite among pizza lovers.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Tomato sauce (1/2 cup)
  • Fresh mozzarella (8 ounces, sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves (a handful)
  • Olive oil (1 tablespoon)
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (250°C). Roll out the pizza dough onto a baking sheet or pizza stone.
  2. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough. Add slices of mozzarella evenly across the top.
  3. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  4. Remove from the oven, top with fresh basil leaves, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with a pinch of salt. Serve immediately.
